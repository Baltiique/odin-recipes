A Delicious Homemade Biriyani

A delicious homemade biryani is a true labor of love, layered with fragrant basmati rice, tender marinated meat, and a blend of spices that fill the kitchen with mouthwatering aromas. Slow-cooked to perfection, every bite bursts with rich flavors — from the warmth of saffron and cardamom to the subtle heat of chilies. Garnished with caramelized onions, fresh coriander, and a squeeze of lemon, this dish is a feast for both the heart and the senses.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of basmati rice
  • 500g chicken (or protein of choice)
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • ½ cup yogurt
  • 2 or 3 green chilies
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • Whole spices (cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, bay leaf)
  • Ground spices (turmeric, chili powder, coriander, garam masala)
  • Fresh coriander & mint leaves
  • ¼ cup oil or ghee
  • Saffron in warm milk (optional)
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Rinse basmati rice and soak for 30 minutes
  2. Heat oil/ghee, fry sliced onions until golden, then set some aside for garnish.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste, tomatoes, yogurt, chilies, and spices; cook until chicken is tender.
  4. Cook rice with whole spices until 70 to 80% done, then drain.
  5. In a pot, layer rice and chicken alternately, sprinkle fried onions, herbs, and saffron milk.
  6. Cover tightly and cook on low heat for 20–25 minutes.
  7. Fluff gently and serve hot with raita or salad
